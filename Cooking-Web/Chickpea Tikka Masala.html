Chickpea Tikka Masala

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ginger paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1/2 medium fresh jalapeño chile pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 3/4 cup water, or as needed
  • 1/4 cup half and half
  • 1/4 cup snipped fresh cilantro
  • 4 cups hot cooked basmati rice

Cooking Instructions:

Step 1: Gather all ingredients.

Step 2: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add finely chopped onions and carrots, cooking them until they reach a tender consistency, typically taking 5 to 6 minutes.

Step 3: Incorporate garam masala, cumin, and black pepper into the mixture, stirring continuously. Cook until the amalgamation emanates a fragrant aroma, typically around 30 seconds.

Step 4: Introduce tomato paste, ginger paste, garlic paste, and diced jalapeño pepper into the skillet. Cook and stir the medley for 2 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and infuse the dish with a delightful blend of spices.

Step 5: Incorporate chickpeas, tomato sauce, sugar, salt, and cayenne pepper into the skillet. Cook over medium-high heat until the mixture is thoroughly heated. Subsequently, reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the dish to reach its optimal savory profile.

Step 6: Blend in 3/4 cup of water and half-and-half, ensuring a smooth consistency. Continue cooking on low heat until the mixture is thoroughly heated through, approximately 1 minute. If you prefer a saucier texture, feel free to add more water as desired. Once heated to perfection, remove the skillet from the heat, cover, and keep the dish warm, preserving its delightful flavors.

Step 7: Top with cilantro and serve with hot cooked basmati rice and/or naan.
