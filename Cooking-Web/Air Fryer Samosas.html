Air Fryer Samosas

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2/3 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro, plus more for garnish
  • 24 wonton wrappers
  • cooking spray

Cooking Instructions:

Step 1: Place the potatoes in a small saucepan and add enough salted water to cover them. Bring the water to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to simmer and cook the potatoes for approximately 10 minutes or until they are easily pierced with a fork. Drain the potatoes and transfer them to a bowl. Mash the potatoes until they reach a mostly smooth consistency, leaving a few small chunks for texture. This process yields a creamy and flavorful mashed potato base, perfect for various dishes.

Step 2: Using the same saucepan, heat oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they become tender, usually taking about 3 to 4 minutes. Introduce freshly grated ginger and minced garlic to the pan; cook until their aromas become fragrant, which typically takes about 30 seconds. Sprinkle in garam masala, cumin, salt, and, if desired, cayenne; cook for an additional 30 seconds to enhance the flavors. Incorporate the previously prepared mashed potatoes and peas into the mixture, ensuring they are thoroughly heated. Once warmed through, remove the pan from heat and gently fold in fresh cilantro for a burst of vibrant flavor.

Step 3: Lay out wonton wrappers on a clean work surface and evenly distribute the filling into the centers of the wrappers. Moisten the edges of the wrappers lightly with water. Fold two opposite corners together, creating triangles, and firmly press the edges to seal the wrappers. Ensure both sides of the samosas are coated with cooking spray for a golden and crisp finish.

Step 4: Preheat the air fryer to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Apply a thin layer of cooking spray to coat the air fryer basket, ensuring an even distribution for optimal results.

Step 5: Working in batches if necessary, place the samosas in an even layer in the basket. Cook, flipping them once halfway through, until the samosas achieve a delightful golden brown and crispy texture, typically around 8 minutes.

Step 6: In the meantime, blend together yogurt, cilantro, lime zest, lime juice, garlic, salt, and pepper in a compact food processor. Process the ingredients until they are thoroughly combined. Refrigerate the mixture, covered, for up to 5 days, allowing the flavors to meld and intensify.

Step 7: Taste samosas with cilantro-yogurt sauce.
